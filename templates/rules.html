Ladder Rules

OA Summer Tennis Ladder — official rules & guidelines

📋

The Ladder

  • 1. The ladder is a ranking system where players compete to move up the rankings by challenging and beating higher-ranked players.
  • 2. Players may only challenge opponents within 5 positions above or below their current ranking.
  • 3. If the winner is ranked lower than the loser, the winner moves up to the loser's position and everyone in between shifts down one place.
  • 4. If the winner is already ranked higher than the loser, positions remain unchanged.
  • 5. You may have a maximum of 2 outgoing challenges active at any one time.
  • 6. Players who do not complete at least one match within 30 days of joining or since their last match will be automatically dropped 10 positions down the ladder. The clock resets after each match played.
  • 7. Players may mark themselves as temporarily unavailable through their profile page (e.g. holidays, injury). Unavailable players cannot be challenged and are immune to the inactivity clock. On returning, they drop 3 places from their current position and the inactivity clock restarts.
  • 8. Players may leave the ladder permanently at any time through their profile page. Active challenges are cancelled and match history is preserved. Players who wish to return will be placed at the bottom of the ladder.
🏆

Prizes

  • 1. At the end of the season, prizes will be awarded to the top 5 players on the final ladder standings.
  • 2. A Most Improved prize will be awarded to the player who climbed the ladder most consistently throughout the season — rewarding sustained progress over the course of the competition, not just a single large jump.
⚡

Challenges

  • 1. Challenges are sent through the website. The challenged player will receive an email notification.
  • 2. The challenged player must accept or decline within 3 days. Failure to respond will result in a walkover win for the challenger and a position change.
  • 3. Once a challenge is accepted, the match must be played within 10 days. If the match is not played in time, the challenge expires with no position change.
  • 4. A player cannot be challenged if they already have an active incoming challenge — challenge the same player again once their current challenge is resolved.
  • 5. You may cancel a pending outgoing challenge before it is accepted, but you cannot cancel once the opponent has accepted.
  • 6. Every player receives a ⚡ wildcard before their very first challenge, then again after every 3 matches played (3rd, 6th, 9th etc.). A wildcard lets you challenge any player on the ladder regardless of their position. It is use-it-or-lose-it — it is replaced when you earn the next one.
🎾

Match Format

  • 1. Matches are played as best of 3 sets. Standard tennis scoring applies.
  • 2. A tie-break (first to 7 points, with a 2-point lead) is played at 6-6 in each of the first two sets.
  • 3. If a deciding third set is required, a match tie-break to 10 points (win by 2) is played instead of a full set.
  • 4. Players are responsible for organising the time and venue of their match between themselves.
✅

Submitting Results

  • 1. Either player may submit the result through the website using the Submit Result page.
  • 2. The score must be entered in the format 6-4 6-2 or 7-5 3-6 10-5 — one set per group, separated by spaces.
  • 3. Submitting a result will automatically close the challenge and update the ladder positions.
  • 4. Results should be submitted promptly after the match is played — please do not leave results unsubmitted.
🤝

Code of Conduct

  • 1. All players are expected to play in a fair and sporting manner. Respect your opponent and the spirit of the game.
  • 2. Players must call lines honestly. In the event of a disputed call, the point should be replayed.
  • 3. Be punctual and responsive. Ignoring challenges or being uncontactable is not acceptable.
  • 4. Any disputes should be raised with Margie Edge.
📍 Challenge players within ±5 positions
